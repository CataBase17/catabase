## Understanding Why Your Cat Is Peeing Outside the Litter Box

Max has been peeing in the same spot despite having three litter boxes available, plenty of scratching poles, toys, and even attempts at the "camping method." Veterinary tests have ruled out any medical conditions, which means this is a behavioral issue. Max is otherwise a chill and affectionate cat who gets along well with people, so something about his environment is triggering this behavior.

## The Real Problem: Feeling Trapped

The root cause is likely related to Max's companion, Gordo, who has a strong temperament and gets upset when Max tries to enter his area. Max's current litter box setup—a high-sided box with a lid—creates a dangerous situation from Max's perspective. Inside that box, he can't see Gordo coming, and he has no escape route if Gordo approaches, even out of curiosity.

When a cat feels cornered or vulnerable, he'll do anything to get out of that situation. Max has figured out that if he pees next to the litter box instead, he maintains a 360-degree view of his surroundings and multiple escape routes. This gives him the sense of control and safety he needs.

The placement of your litter boxes reinforces this problem. Even with one lidded and one open box available, if they're positioned in a way that still leaves Max feeling trapped by Gordo's proximity, he'll continue to avoid them.

## How to Fix It

Start by experimenting with a low-sided, regular litter box without a lid. Place it in the area where Max has been peeing, and put a mat underneath to catch any accidents while you're testing this setup.

The general rule for litter boxes is one per cat, plus one extra—so for two cats, you need three boxes. The key is to spread them throughout your house rather than clustering them together. Consider placing one in a central location like your living room, where Max can feel more in control and secure because there are escape routes available. It's better to have the litter box out in the open rather than tucked away in a corner or against a wall.

A surveillance camera can be helpful for monitoring Max's behavior and seeing exactly what's happening when Gordo approaches. This will help you understand whether your changes are working and identify any other stressors you might have missed.
