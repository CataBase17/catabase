# Yeowww! Yellow Banana

Yeowww! makes high-quality catnip toys with breathable canvas that maintains the catnip's potency even as it gradually fades over time. The Yeowww! Yellow Banana is a popular choice among cat owners and is relatively inexpensive, making it a great stocking stuffer or low-cost way to test whether your cat responds to catnip.

# Catstages Tower of Tracks

The Catstages Tower of Tracks features balls that roll around in tracks and is a worthwhile investment for stimulating cats. It's suitable for kittens, but grown cats tend to lose interest quickly since the toy isn't complex and they master it fast.

Rather than leaving it out all the time, try putting it away and bringing it back after a week or two. This rotation keeps it feeling fresh and more engaging for your cat, even if she's already figured out how it works.

# Fashion's Talk Mouse Cat Toys Mixed Bag

This mixed bag contains 27 assorted mice toys with catnip, feathers, and furry rattles. At around $3.39 for 27 toys, the price is tempting, but quality can be compromised. A significant issue is that the feather tails detach easily when your cat bites them, scattering feathers around the house and creating a cleanup problem. Your cat could also accidentally ingest loose feathers.

The positive side is that these toys don't have small beady eyeballs that pose a choking hazard. The mice will likely end up under furniture or lost around the house, but they still provide entertainment value.

To make them more appealing, you can marinate the toys in catnip. The porous material readily absorbs and holds onto catnip, making the toys more stimulating for your cat's senses.

# SmartyKat Dazzle Unicorn Crinkle Plush Catnip Cat Toy

The SmartyKat Dazzle Unicorn claims to contain catnip, but the smell is barely noticeable—if at all. The toy comes with a ribbon attached, which is a significant choking hazard. Remove and discard the ribbon before giving the toy to your cat, leaving behind what you might call a "naked unicorn."

The weak catnip smell likely results from the catnip being old and ineffective. Shipping from China can take 90 days, which causes catnip to lose its potency during transit. # Cat Toys Snake Interactive

This USB rechargeable snake moves on wheels and simulates realistic motion. Once turned on, it moves in different directions after sensing obstacles, though this feature isn't always reliable. The wheel design doesn't work well on carpet, so this toy is best used on hard floors.

# Catstages by Nina Ottosson Buggin' Out Puzzle & Play

Every cat should have at least one puzzle toy in the house, and this highly-rated option is an excellent choice. It has adjustable difficulty levels to accommodate your cat's learning and problem-solving skills.

The toy works by placing a treat inside. Your cat must move pieces around or manipulate the little leaves to retrieve the treat. This provides mental stimulation and encourages her to work for her food, using her brain while eating. One minor flaw is that treats can get stuck between the two pieces of plastic, requiring some effort to clean out, but this doesn't diminish the toy's benefits.

Puzzle toys are amazing for keeping cats engaged, especially treat-motivated cats who can solve them and keep themselves busy hunting for rewards. Combined with the other toys in a rotation, puzzle toys help provide the variety and stimulation your cat needs.

# Keeping Your Cat Engaged

Rather than leaving the same toys out constantly, rotate them regularly. Put toys away and bring them back after a week or two so they seem new and exciting again. This simple strategy keeps your cat engaged and interested in her toy collection, even when the toys themselves haven't changed.

With a variety of toy types—interactive toys, puzzle toys, catnip toys, and motion toys—your cat will have options for different moods and energy levels throughout the day.
