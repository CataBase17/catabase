## Why Your Cat Digs in Plant Dirt

Your cat's fascination with digging in potted plants is actually pretty common—and while it might seem adorable, there are good reasons to discourage it. Potting soil often contains fertilizers and growth agents that can be harmful to your cat. Beyond the health concern, there's also the practical issue of dirt scattered all over your home.

## Making Plants Less Appealing

The goal is to strike a balance: say "no" to the potted plants while saying "yes" to safer alternatives that let your cat enjoy digging.

One effective approach is double-sided tape like Sticky Paws placed around the soil surface. Your cat will dislike the sticky feeling on her paws and will learn to avoid the pot—and the tape won't harm the plant.

You can also use lava rocks placed on top of the soil in your pots. This keeps the dirt out of reach while still allowing your plant to get the nutrients it needs. The rocks make digging unappealing without blocking your plant's access to water and air.

## Offering Safe Alternatives

The key to success is giving your cat her own approved outlets for this natural behavior. Cat grass is an excellent choice—it's easy to plant and maintain, and it gives your cat a safe, dedicated space to dig and nibble. You can set it up in its own container right next to your other plants.

Catnip is another option to plant in the same area, offering additional enrichment alongside the cat grass. For the ultimate digging experience, consider giving your cat her own tub or container filled with safe, clean dirt. This dedicated space lets her satisfy her natural urge to dig without threatening your houseplants.

The combination of deterrents on your plants and enticing alternatives nearby teaches your cat where she can and can't dig—and gives her something even more fun to do instead.
