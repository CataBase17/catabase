## Nutrition for Senior Cats

Nutrition becomes increasingly important as your cat ages. Moisture-rich food is especially beneficial because cats naturally get up to 80 percent of their hydration through food. Since older cats may not drink as much water on their own, wet food helps ensure they stay properly hydrated.

A natural, bio-appropriate diet based on real meat is ideal for senior cats. This type of diet allows your cat's body to filter out impurities—like the carbohydrates found in dry food—so it can function properly. A meat-rich diet, combined with appropriate supplements, can support bone health, joint health, and cognitive function as your cat enters her senior years.

## Keeping Your Senior Cat Active

It's easy to assume that a very old cat should just rest, but gentle mental and physical engagement remains important throughout her life. This helps your cat stay connected to her natural instincts and remain vital.

For a senior cat, active engagement doesn't mean high-energy running or jumping. Instead, consider passive activities like watching bird feeders outside the window or "cat TV" videos. Even gentle play—following a feather toy at a slow pace—can be stimulating and beneficial. Many guardians of geriatric cats make the mistake of stopping play altogether, but even modest, low-key play sessions help keep an older cat mentally and physically engaged.

## Regular Veterinary Care

Health issues can develop quickly in senior cats, even when they appear to be doing well and eating normally. This is why more frequent vet visits become essential once your cat reaches 19 or 20 years old.

Aim for blood tests twice a year to catch potential problems early. Pay close attention to your cat's litter box habits—any unusual changes are worth reporting to your vet. Arthritis is particularly common in cats at this age, affecting a large percentage of senior felines, though it often goes undiagnosed.

Beyond traditional veterinary care, complementary therapies can help manage age-related conditions. Acupuncture, cold laser treatment, and targeted supplements have all shown promise in keeping senior cats comfortable and vital. These approaches can make a significant difference in your cat's quality of life in her later years.
