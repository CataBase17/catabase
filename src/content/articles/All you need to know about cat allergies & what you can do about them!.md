## Understanding Suckling Behavior in Cats

Your older cat may have a habit of suckling on blankets, clothing, or other soft items whenever she settles in with you. This behavior is often a leftover from early weaning—when a kitten is separated from her mother too soon. For your cat, suckling serves as a self-soothing mechanism, similar to how a human child might suck their thumb. Once a cat develops this habit, it can persist into adulthood and become deeply ingrained over the years.

## Is It Pica or Just Suckling?

Before you decide how to manage the behavior, it's important to determine whether your cat is actually ingesting the materials or simply suckling on them. Pica is the condition where cats eat non-organic materials—things like sweaters, dresser knobs, or even wall trim. Cats with pica continuously chew and swallow these items, which can be dangerous.

If your cat is just suckling without swallowing, the main concern is keeping her comfortable. However, if she is ingesting materials, this becomes a health risk. Certain items like yarn or string can wrap around the intestinal tract and cause serious harm. Watch carefully to see whether materials are being damaged or whether your cat is simply wetting items with saliva.

## Managing the Behavior at Home

Changing a deeply ingrained behavior in a senior cat is often not practical or worth the effort. At 21 years old, your cat has likely had this habit for most of her life, and teaching her to stop may be more stressful than beneficial for both of you.

Instead, focus on managing your environment. The most effective approach is to "cat-proof" your home by removing or securing the items your cat likes to suckle on. Store cable knit sweaters, throw rugs, and blankets away when you're not using them, or keep them in sealed containers. If your cat can't access these items, the behavior becomes much less of a problem—even if she does manage to suckle on something occasionally and leave it damp.

By adjusting what's available to her rather than trying to change her behavior, you'll reduce frustration for yourself while allowing your cat to maintain a comforting habit that brings her peace.
