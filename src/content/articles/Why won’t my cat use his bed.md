## Why Your Cat Won't Use Their Bed

If you've invested in a beautiful new bed for your cat, only to watch them ignore it in favor of a cardboard box or the corner of your couch, you're not alone. Understanding why your cat rejects their bed is the first step toward finding one they'll actually use.

## Texture Matters More Than You Think

Your cat's preferences for bed texture may be quite different from what appeals to you. Cats can be drawn to fleece, but they might equally prefer cold, hard surfaces—especially in warm weather. Some cats love unconventional textures like astroturf or corrugated cardboard, while others will choose to lie on cement or tile if given the option.

You can figure out your cat's texture preference by observing their natural behavior. Does your cat like to lie on top of a horizontal scratcher? Do they gravitate toward corrugated surfaces or smooth floors? These clues tell you what tactile experience your cat is seeking. Rather than guessing, try introducing beds with different textures and watch which ones catch their interest.

## Getting to Know Your Individual Cat

Learning what your cat likes is similar to getting to know a new person—it takes time and observation. Since you can't simply ask your cat about their preferences, you'll need to experiment and pay attention to their choices.

Start with less expensive options when you're still figuring out what your cat wants. There's no point investing heavily in a luxurious bed if your cat's actual preference is for something simple and inexpensive. Focus on texture and feel rather than what looks good to you.

Be patient with this process. It's all part of building a deeper relationship with your cat and understanding their unique personality.

## Shape and Size Make a Difference

Just as cats can be particular about litter box shape, they can be equally selective about bed shape. Some cats prefer enclosed, hooded beds that make them feel secure. Others want a completely flat surface. Some like round beds, while others prefer rectangular ones or beds designed to fit on top of furniture.

The best approach is to start with one bed option and observe your cat's response before buying more. This helps you learn whether your cat is a "tree dweller" who likes height, a "bush dweller" who prefers enclosed spaces, or a "beach dweller" who likes flat, open areas. Understanding these preferences lets you create an environment that genuinely suits your cat's needs.

## Location Is Critical

Where you place the bed matters as much as what the bed is. Cats have strong preferences about their vertical position—some want to rest on the floor, others prefer a certain height, and some love the top of the refrigerator.

Sunny spots are particularly appealing to cats, who naturally follow the sun throughout the day. These warm patches are often areas of real social significance in a home, especially if you have multiple cats. Placing a bed in a sunny window or on a sunlit shelf can greatly increase the odds your cat will use it.

You should also consider competition. If you have dogs or children, your cat may need a bed placed in a spot where they can have complete privacy and peace. Cats value sole ownership of their territory.

Give your cat time to settle into a new bed location. It's tempting to move a rejected bed to a different spot, but constantly relocating it prevents your cat from feeling a sense of ownership. Let a new bed stay in one place for at least a month before deciding it's not working. Cats need time to become accustomed to a space and claim it as their own. Territory ownership is what gives cats their confidence and well-being.

## Keep Experimenting

Your cat's bed preference is worth discovering because you'll use this knowledge throughout their life. Try different combinations of location, texture, size, and shape. Don't give up after one or two attempts.

Remember that your cat's confidence and contentment come from feeling they own their territory. By providing beds in various locations and styles, and allowing your cat time to establish ownership of the spaces that appeal to them, you're giving them exactly what they need to feel secure and happy in your home.
