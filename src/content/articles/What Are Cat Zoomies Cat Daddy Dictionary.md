## What Are Zoomies?

Zoomies is a behavior characterized by sudden bursts of running in unpredictable directions with no clear destination. You might also hear it called frantic random activity periods (or FRAPs). This is the phenomenon that often strikes without warning, frequently in the middle of the night, disrupting your sleep and sending your cat careening through your home. While zoomies might seem innocent and entertaining on the surface, they can actually have a destructive side that's worth understanding.

## Why Your Cat Gets Zoomies

Your cat's energy builds up throughout the day. If this pent-up energy isn't released in a structured way, it explodes into those wild, seemingly random bursts of activity. Think of it as a pressure that needs an outlet—without one, your cat will find chaotic ways to release it.

## How to Prevent Zoomies

The key to preventing zoomies is establishing what you might call the 3 R's: **routine, ritual, and rhythm**. These three elements give your cat a predictable outlet for their raw energy and help align their behavior with your own sleep schedule.

The most effective approach is to follow a hunt, catch, kill, eat, groom, sleep rhythm. This mirrors your cat's natural instincts and body clock. A practical way to implement this is to commit to a consistent playtime each day, followed by feeding your cat after the play session. This combination helps your cat develop a circadian rhythm—a body clock similar to the one humans have.

By providing a structured daily routine with scheduled play and feeding times, you give your cat a way to release their energy in a predictable and manageable way. When your cat's routine aligns with your own sleep schedule, you'll notice a significant reduction in those middle-of-the-night zoomies.
