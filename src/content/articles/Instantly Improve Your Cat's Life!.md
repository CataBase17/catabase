## What is Catification?

Catification is the process of creating an environment that enriches your cat's life while also being aesthetically pleasing and functional for you. It's about finding a balance between your cat's needs and your own budget, style, and preferences. Environmental enrichment is at the heart of catification—it's essential for your cat's well-being and involves thoughtful changes to how you and your cat share your home.

## The Importance of Enrichment

Your cat's behavior and instincts are rooted in their wild ancestor, and understanding this connection helps you improve their life. One of the most important things for your cat's happiness is a sense of territorial ownership. For cats, this doesn't just mean claiming a spot on the floor—it means owning the entire vertical space from floor to ceiling. Cats have a natural instinct to "own the sky," and catification is about uniting everything from the floor to the ceiling, creating opportunities throughout your space where your cat can feel confident and in control of their territory.

## Getting Started Without Overthinking It

Many people feel paralyzed by the idea of catification, thinking it requires a massive budget and professional help. The reality is that you can start small and make a real difference. Perfection shouldn't be the enemy of good—begin with a simple, achievable goal, such as creating one catified area in your home. Think of it as "planting the flag of catification" and expanding from there.

When you're planning your space, consider how your cat will move through it. Think like an urban planner: What are the traffic patterns? Where are the important areas? How can both you and your cat navigate the space comfortably? One useful concept is the "Cat Super Highway"—the paths and routes your cat naturally takes through your home. You want to create an "on-ramp" or entry point to this highway so your cat can use it easily.

## Windows: The Foundation of Catification

Windows are a perfect starting point for catification. Cats are naturally drawn to sunny spots, and windows provide warmth and vitamin D that cats find comforting. Beyond that, windows offer "Cat TV"—a constant stream of entertainment from birds, critters, and movement outside. You can enhance this by setting up bird feeders or bird baths outside your windows, creating an engaging experience that stimulates your cat's natural hunting instincts.

Even watching outdoor activity is enriching for your cat, offering mental and emotional stimulation as they exercise their instincts from a safe vantage point.

## How to Catify Your Windows

The good news is that you can catify a window at any budget level. Start with the simplest approach: move a chair or piece of furniture to the window and let your cat enjoy the view. Adding a favorite blanket or a comfortable "chill mat" makes it even nicer.

As you consider your cat's needs, think about their physical capabilities. Senior cats or cats with mobility challenges may need a ramp to reach a window perch—something as simple as a sturdy 2x4 can work well.

If you want to invest a little, products like window sills or pet steps are available, though you should read reviews to ensure they're sturdy enough for your cat's weight. Cardboard loungers are another affordable option; they're made from strong corrugated cardboard, absorb scent, and become a comforting "scent soaker" for your cat.

A cat tree placed near a window is also excellent. It doesn't need to be expensive or floor-to-ceiling—just functional and placed where your cat can watch the world outside.

The key is providing a space that meets your cat's needs without feeling overwhelmed or striving for perfection.

## Building Your Cat Super Highway

Once your window is catified, think of it as the center of an urban planning project. Your window becomes the on-ramp for the Cat Super Highway—a system that allows your cat to traverse your entire room without needing to touch the floor. This is the beginning of creating a cat-friendly environment where your cat can move freely and confidently throughout your space.

Start with your window, make small changes, and you'll be amazed at how much you can improve your cat's life and overall well-being.
