## How Many Litter Boxes You Need

The general rule is to have one litter box per cat, plus one extra. So if you have two cats, you should have three boxes; if you have three cats, you should have four. This helps ensure that your cats can territorially identify the entire home.

## Where to Place Litter Boxes

Place litter boxes in socially important areas of your home, such as the living room or dining room, rather than in secluded spots like the garage or mud room. Cats don't actually need privacy when using the litter box—in fact, they naturally want to mark their territory in visible areas. This placement strategy can help prevent litter box problems.

If your cat is having litter box issues, you may need to make a choice that goes against your aesthetic instincts. Place boxes in areas where your cat feels insecure, and praise them for using them correctly.

## Litter Box Design

Covered litter boxes with hoods that face the wall can be problematic. Your cat needs to feel like they have an escape route and be able to see their surroundings. Position the hood opening so your cat can look around while using the box—this helps them avoid feeling trapped and protects them from being ambushed.

When placing litter boxes around your home, make sure your cat has clear exit routes everywhere. This is especially important if your cat has had negative experiences with litter boxes before, such as being ambushed by another cat or experiencing pain while using the box.

## Keeping Litter Boxes Clean

Skip plug-in deodorizers, sprinkle-on deodorizers, and scented litter—these products can actually drive cats away from the box. Instead, consistently scoop out your cat's waste. Regular scooping is the most effective way to prevent odors and keep your cat happy to use the box.

## Introducing New Litter Boxes

If your cat is struggling with their current setup, introduce new litter boxes that look, feel, and smell different from the existing ones. Vary the shapes, depths, and types of litter, and let your cats choose which ones they prefer. You don't need to remove the old boxes right away—instead, add new options and gradually replace the old ones that may be causing stress or discomfort.

## Creating an Enriching Environment

Environmental enrichment for your cat can help prevent litter box problems. The good news is that you can create an environment that works well for both your cats and your home, without compromising your own sense of style.
