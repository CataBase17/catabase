## Understanding Your Adult Cat's Life Stages

When your cat reaches around two to three years old, they've generally settled into their life. The intense energy and rapid development of kittenhood starts to calm down. By the end of the second year, your cat is roughly equivalent to a 25-year-old human. During the prime years—around three to six years old—your cat is in their 40s in human years. As they move into their mature phase from seven to ten years old, they're equivalent to a human in their mid-50s.

## The Importance of Daily Play and Exercise

As your cat transitions into adulthood, their spontaneous bursts of energy from their kitten and teenage years slow down. Now it's your job to be their playmate and keep them physically and behaviorally vital through daily exercise. This is just as important as nutrition for their overall health.

Your cat's need for adventure and excitement doesn't diminish with age—it's up to you to create opportunities for play. Vary the toys and activities you offer, since using the same toy repeatedly becomes boring. Mix up running, jumping, and hunting games to keep the relationship fresh and exciting. This prevents them from becoming lethargic and developing unhealthy habits.

## Feeding Your Adult Cat Well

Nutrition plays a crucial role as your cat's metabolism slows down during adulthood. Instead of free-feeding, establish a routine where your cat eats meals at set times. This helps maintain their physical health and circadian rhythm, which is key to their overall well-being.

Since cats are obligate carnivores, feeding wet food rather than dry food is recommended. A diet high in meat content and hydration sets them up for a long, healthy life. As cats enter adulthood, their activity level decreases, making them more prone to obesity, which can lead to health issues like diabetes.

Variety in your cat's diet is also essential. Eating the same food with the same flavor and texture throughout their life can lead to boredom and make it harder to introduce new foods as they age. By establishing a balanced and varied diet early on, you reduce the risk of health issues like diabetes and kidney disease, which often become more prevalent around age seven.

## Creating an Enriching Environment

Environmental enrichment is crucial for indoor cats as they move through adulthood. Provide opportunities for your cat to get vertical, have choices, and engage in activities like watching birds or following the sun around your house.

Rather than just having blankets or beds on the floor, set up shelves, cat trees, or beds that face windows. This allows your cat to climb, search, and exercise. Vertical catification keeps cats active and can be adapted to their changing needs as they age—you can remove a shelf to make them jump harder or add one back to make it easier for them to walk across.

The prime years from three to six years old are a critical time to establish these enrichment habits. Once your cat reaches their mature years, it becomes more challenging to start new activities, and even harder after they enter their senior years.

## The Prime Years vs. the Mature Years

The difference between your cat's prime years (three to six) and mature years (seven to ten) isn't dramatic, except for a noticeable decrease in activity level. As your cat ages, they become more sedentary and ritualistically tied to their daily routines.

During the mature phase, monitor your cat's body for signs of wear and tear—like avoiding jumping up or down. Address potential issues like arthritis through conventional treatment or alternative therapies such as acupuncture. The goal is to prevent compensation and breakdown of your cat's entire system by keeping them active and engaged.

Adjust your cat's play and activities to accommodate their changing abilities. If your cat can't jump as high anymore, try playing on the floor instead. Provide "cat TV" by allowing them to watch the outside world, which keeps their hunter mind sharp and provides mental stimulation without requiring physical exertion.

## Growing Old Together

The relationship between you and your cat is the most important thing. All the tools and activities—exercise, nutrition, environmental enrichment, and body monitoring—are contained within that relationship, which is the key to growing old together with your cat. By finding new challenges, dialing in nutrition, and guiding your cat through life in a healthy way, you help prevent the decline of their physical and mental health during their mature years and beyond.
