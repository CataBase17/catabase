The holiday season brings celebrations and festivities, but the loud noises from fireworks and parties can be stressful and overwhelming for your cat. Taking steps to keep your cat safe and comfortable during these times is essential for their well-being.

## Create a safe base camp for your cat

Start by designating a quiet room where your cat can retreat during noisy celebrations. This room should feel familiar and secure—the master bedroom works well. Stock it with items that comfort your cat, such as their favorite blankets and toys. Include something with your scent, like a worn sweatshirt, which can be very reassuring.

Make sure the room has heavy curtains or blackout curtains to help block out visual stimulation and reduce the impact of flashing lights. This space becomes your cat's safe haven where they can retreat when things feel overwhelming.

## Mask disturbing sounds

Close the doors to your cat's safe room to minimize noise from outside. Use white noise machines, radios at low volume, or other devices that produce constant, soothing background sounds to mask the loud noises of fireworks and parties. These steady sounds can help your cat feel more at ease.

Depending on how sensitive your cat is to loud noises, additional support may help. Holistic remedies can help reduce stress and lower your cat's overall anxiety during the event. If your cat is extremely sensitive to loud noises, talk to your veterinarian about whether medication might help them rest comfortably through the night. It's not worth letting your cat suffer through a distressing experience if there are options available.

## Ensure proper identification

Despite your best efforts to create a safe space, frightened cats can sometimes escape. Make sure your cat is wearing a breakaway collar with a tag that has current contact information. More importantly, have your cat microchipped. Microchipping significantly increases the chances of your cat being returned if they get lost, but only if the microchip is registered and connected to current phone numbers and addresses. Check that your microchip information is up to date before the holidays arrive.

## Keep photos ready

Take clear, full-frontal photographs of your cat now, before the holidays. If your cat does escape, you'll have these images ready to make lost-pet signs and alert your neighborhood immediately. Having these pictures on hand can make a real difference in bringing your cat home safely.
