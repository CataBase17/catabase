## Why Your Cat Rushes Out of the Litter Box

If your cat is bolting out of the litter box right after using it and leaving poop stains on your carpet or floors, this behavior usually signals discomfort. Your cat isn't being difficult—something is making him want to get away from the litter box as quickly as possible.

## Start with a Vet Visit

The first step is to rule out medical issues. Your cat's rushing behavior may stem from pain or discomfort while eliminating, not from a behavioral problem. If his stools are too hard, too soft, or if something else is going on, he'll associate the litter box with that discomfort and flee. A vet can examine him and help identify what's really happening.

## Check for Parasites

If your cat was rescued from the street, there's a good chance he picked up parasites. Hookworms, roundworms, tapeworms, coccidia, and giardia can all cause digestive distress and irregular stools. Your cat may have contracted these by eating infected rodents, drinking dirty water, or picking up fleas. Ask your vet to check a fecal sample. Deworming medication is very effective at eliminating these parasites and helping your cat feel better.

## Evaluate Your Cat's Diet

Food intolerances and allergies are common culprits. Many cats have sensitivities to the dominant protein in their food, whether that's chicken, beef, or fish. Once your vet gives your cat a clean bill of health, consider a food trial using a limited ingredient diet with a novel protein like rabbit or venison. It typically takes around six weeks to determine if a food change will help.

While you're sorting out diet, you can also support your cat's digestive system with supplements like Slippery Elm to soothe his gut, and probiotics to support overall gut health.

## Check Your Litter Box Setup

Remove the lid from your litter box if it has one. Covered boxes can make cats feel uncomfortable and trapped, which creates a negative association with the litter box itself. There's no harm in removing it, and it may help your cat feel more at ease.
