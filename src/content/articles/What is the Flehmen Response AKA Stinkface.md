## Understanding the Flehmen Response

You've probably noticed your cat making an odd face—curling back their upper lip, exposing their front teeth, and inhaling deeply with their nostrils usually closed. It might look like your cat is angry or disgusted, but there's actually a scientific explanation for this behavior, called the flehmen response, or as many cat owners affectionately call it, "stinkface."

The flehmen response gets its name from an upper German phrase meaning "to bear teeth" or "to look spiteful," though the behavior itself has nothing to do with anger or judgment. When your cat does this, they're typically holding their neck stretched upward and inhaling for several seconds. This isn't a reaction unique to cats—horses, zebras, elk, tapirs, and elephants all display this same behavior.

## How Your Cat Uses the Flehmen Response

When your cat makes that stinkface, they're gathering important information about their environment through something called the Jacobson's organ. This special sensory organ helps your cat process pheromones—chemical signals that communicate vital information, especially about other cats' reproductive status.

You'll often see your cat do this when they encounter scent markings on the ground, particularly from another cat's urine. Male cats are especially prone to this behavior when picking up these signals. In homes with multiple cats, one cat might display the flehmen response to alert you that something unusual is present on a surface—essentially signaling that there's a strange scent or substance that doesn't belong there.

While the flehmen response most commonly helps your cat gather information about other cats, evidence suggests your cat can also use it to process scents from other species.

## What This Behavior Really Means

The most important thing to understand is that the stinkface has absolutely nothing to do with dislike or spite toward you. When your cat makes that face, they're not being judgmental or rejecting you. It's simply a natural behavior that helps your cat understand and communicate about their world. So even though it might look funny or even slightly unpleasant to human eyes, it's actually a sign that your cat is doing exactly what they're supposed to do—being a cat and gathering information about their surroundings.
