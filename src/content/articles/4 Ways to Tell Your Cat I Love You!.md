## The Slow Blink: Your Cat's "I Love You"

The slow blink is one of the most meaningful ways to communicate affection to your cat. When you slowly close and open your eyes while looking at your cat, you're speaking their language of trust and vulnerability.

Cats are naturally prey animals, which means they're always alert to danger. They often sleep with one eye open for safety. When a cat closes their eyes in a slow blink, it's a significant gesture—they're showing you they feel safe and comfortable enough to be vulnerable around you. This is why the slow blink is such a powerful expression of trust between you and your cat.

To practice the slow blink with your cat, relax your face and soften your eyes. Think the words "I love you" as you slowly close and open your eyes. Take your time with the gesture. When your cat slow blinks back at you, it's a sign of affection. Respond calmly and gently to reinforce that you're trustworthy. This simple exchange strengthens the bond between you and your cat and helps you communicate in a way that feels natural to them.

## Greeting Your Cat

When you come home or walk into a room where your cat is, they often greet you with a soft vocalization or movement—their way of saying "how ya doin'?" You can mirror this greeting by combining the slow blink with a friendly "how ya doin'" as you approach your cat. It might feel a little silly at first, but it's a genuine way to acknowledge and connect with your cat.

## The Bow

Bowing to your cat is another subtle way to express love and respect. You don't need to bow deeply—even a gentle, slight bow accompanied by the thought of your love is a meaningful gesture. Like the slow blink, the bow is a understated but genuine expression of affection that shows your cat you honor them.

## The 3-Step Handshake

The 3-step handshake is a respectful way to ask permission before petting your cat. It puts your cat in control and honors their boundaries.

**Step one:** Start with a slow blink or a greeting like "how ya doin'?" This initiates the interaction and shows your cat you're approaching with calm intention.

**Step two:** Present your hand or a finger for your cat to explore and sniff. This gives your cat a chance to become familiar with your scent and build trust before any touching happens.

**Step three:** Offer your relaxed hand to your cat in a gentle, nose-to-nose greeting position. Let your cat approach at their own pace. They may sniff your hand, nuzzle it, or rub against it—all signs of affection and trust.

When you use the 3-step handshake, your cat learns that you respect their space and boundaries. Some cats will allow you to pet them after this exchange, and some may even initiate contact by pushing their nose against your hand or face. By taking the time to ask permission this way, you're communicating that you understand and value your cat's needs. This deepens your connection and shows your cat that you truly listen to them.
