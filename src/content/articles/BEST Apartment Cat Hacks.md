## Making Your Rental Cat-Friendly

If you rent your home, you might think you're limited in how much you can do for your cat. The good news is that creating a wonderful environment for your cat doesn't require permanent changes or lots of space. You can catify your apartment in ways that work for both you and your cat, respecting the constraints of rental living while giving your cat the enrichment they need.

## Scratching Surfaces That Blend In

Your cat needs to scratch—it's how they exercise their muscles, maintain their claws, and leave their scent around your home. The key is choosing scratching surfaces that work with your space.

Think about how your cat likes to scratch. Many cats prefer horizontal scratching surfaces, which are easy to place discreetly around your apartment—under furniture, near the couch, or by your bed. If your cat scratches at an angle, a triangular or angled scratcher can be positioned the same way, tucked underneath things so it doesn't dominate your décor.

The ideal scratching surface should let your cat reach up the full length of their body to pull down and exercise. Good locations are near areas where your cat already spends time and near furniture where they want to leave their scent alongside yours.

## Creating a Vertical Highway

Cats experience their world vertically, from floor to ceiling. By using existing structures in your apartment, you can create what's called a "cat superhighway"—a connected network where your cat can move around the room without touching the floor.

Start with what you already have: fireplaces, mantles, shelves, and bookcases all become hubs in this network. A mantle works especially well as a central hub. Cat trees can serve as on-ramps to get your cat up into this system.

If you want to add more without drilling into walls, tension-mounted cat climbing poles are perfect for rental apartments. They mount securely between floor and ceiling, requiring no permanent installation. Decorative ladders—especially old or antique-looking ones—also work beautifully as both décor and functional climbing structures that give your cat access to the rest of your vertical network.

## Making Spaces Multi-Purpose

Every piece of furniture in your apartment can serve double duty for your cat's comfort.

Beds are a good example. If your cat loves hiding under them, storage containers slid partway underneath can block off that space while giving you extra storage. This matters because cats shouldn't have unrestricted access under beds—it can be dangerous in emergencies and puts them in control rather than you.

Cat carriers often get put away, but keep yours accessible and ready. Remove the top and turn it into a cozy den or bed. Your cat will feel safe in it, and it's always prepared if you need to transport your cat quickly.

Think about "Sense Soakers"—items where your cat can deposit and soak up their own scent. Cat beds, blankets, and mats work well and can move from place to place as your needs change. Most importantly, don't skip on litter boxes. They're the ultimate scent soaker, and you should have multiple boxes, especially if you have multiple cats. Litter boxes let your cat smell their own scent and feel confident and secure in their space.

## Bringing the Outdoors Inside

Apartment living means limited outdoor access, but you can bring some of that outside feeling indoors.

Cat grass is an easy addition. You can buy a flat of wheat grass from health food stores. Place it near a window where your cat can sit in the sun, chew on it, and roll around in it. The chlorophyll in cat grass aids digestion, making it a healthy choice.

A cat fountain with running water brings another outdoor element inside. Cats love to watch the water, play with it, and drink from it. The light dancing off the water creates a naturally engaging atmosphere that keeps your cat entertained.

## The Bigger Picture

When you view environmental enrichment as a necessity rather than a luxury, you'll find creative ways to make it happen. Cats thrive in vertical spaces, and the more you provide shelves, climbing areas, and vertical networks, the better they'll behave and the happier they'll become. Your cat will truly become the best version of themselves.
