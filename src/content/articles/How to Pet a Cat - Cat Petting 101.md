When you first meet a cat, the most important thing to remember is that cats have wide personal space bubbles. You should respect this boundary and avoid reaching out to touch them right away. Cats appreciate having control over when and how contact happens with you.

## Let Your Cat Make the First Move

The best way to build trust with a new cat is to let them approach you. Offer a "handshake" by holding out your hand and allowing the cat to sniff you and rub against you at their own pace. This gives your cat the choice about contact and helps them feel safe around you.

A helpful technique is the "Michelangelo" move: simply extend one finger and let the cat guide it with their head or rub. This puts your cat in control and is an effective way to build trust while petting.

## Where Cats Prefer to Be Touched

Most cats enjoy being petted on the chin, neck, and top of the head. These areas are generally safe choices, especially with cats you don't know well. You should avoid full-body pets with stranger cats, as stroking their whole body can lead to unwanted reactions or overstimulation.

Actions like placing your hand over the top of a cat's head or rubbing their entire body can actually be seen as insults to a cat. Always ask permission—through your cat's body language and responses—before petting them.

## Building a Stronger Bond

Once your cat trusts you, you may discover specific spots that are particularly enjoyable for them to be petted. Some of these areas, such as certain acupressure points, can be especially rewarding for your cat and help strengthen your bond together.
