Your cat's sudden pounces on your ankles aren't personal—they're hunting. Cats are natural predators with a strong prey drive, and what feels like an attack is actually play aggression, a completely normal feline behavior. When your cat lunges at your moving feet, they're reacting to the movement and treating your ankles like prey.

Your ankles are particularly tempting targets because they resemble the small animals your cat would hunt in the wild. The problem gets worse when you're walking past furniture like tables or beds, which give your cat the perfect hiding spot to launch an ambush. These cats—sometimes called "bush dwellers"—love to hide and pounce, mimicking how they'd hunt prey in nature.

## The Solution: Regular, Structured Play

The most effective way to reduce ankle attacks is to give your cat an outlet for their hunting instincts through regular, interactive play. Use toys like long poles with strings or feathers attached. These toys let you simulate the movement of prey and keep your cat engaged in a more appropriate way than targeting your feet.

Structure your playtime to tire your cat out. A fatigued cat is less likely to ambush your ankles out of boredom or excess energy. Consistency matters—establish a regular play routine so your cat knows when to expect this stimulation and can burn off their natural hunting drive on schedule.

## Enriching Your Home

Beyond playtime, make your environment work for your cat's natural instincts. Provide hiding spots under tables or beds where they can crouch and watch. Offer a variety of toys that can be waved around, hidden, and stalked. Rotating different toys keeps things fresh and interesting.

By catering to your cat's need to hunt and providing plenty of engaging play, you'll see a significant reduction in ankle-attacking behavior and build a stronger, more harmonious relationship with your pet.
