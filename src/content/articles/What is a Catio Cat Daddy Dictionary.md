## What Is a Catio?

A catio is a structure—either permanent or temporary—designed to give your cat a safe outdoor experience. It confines your cat to a designated space while letting them enjoy fresh air, natural light, and outdoor sights and sounds without the dangers of roaming free.

## Why Build One?

A catio lets your cat experience the outdoors in a way that keeps them absolutely safe. Whether you live in an apartment, house, townhome, or even a camper, a catio can significantly improve your cat's happiness and overall well-being.

## Safety First

Safety is the most important consideration when building a catio. Here are the key measures to take:

**Prevent digging.** Place planters with lava rock in front of the catio and extend fencing below ground to stop raccoons and other animals from digging underneath.

**Use heavy-duty wire.** The gauge of your wire needs to be thick enough to withstand pressure from animals like deer. Heavy-duty gauge wire will keep your cats secure while allowing visibility.

**Cover the top.** A ceiling prevents birds of prey from reaching your cats.

**Install a double-door system.** Having one door close while another opens prevents your cat from dashing out into the backyard. This small detail makes a significant safety difference.

## Making It Useful for Your Cat

Your catio should provide plenty of enrichment. Include both vertical and ground-level elements so your cat can climb, perch, rest in sun, and retreat to shade. This mimics what you'd create in an enriching indoor space.

**Add safe plants.** Choose non-toxic options like wheatgrass, cat grass, catnip, or cat mint, which you can plant in containers. Many common plants are toxic to cats, so research what's safe before adding greenery.

**Ensure good footing.** If you include ramps, use sandpapery coatings to prevent slipping in wet conditions.

**Provide something interesting to watch.** Hang a bird feeder visible from the catio so your cat can enjoy "cat TV"—watching birds and other outdoor activity.

## Practical Design Matters

Think about how you'll use the space too. You need to be able to easily scoop the litter box, water plants, and maintain the area without it becoming a burden. A catio shouldn't be a pain to take care of.

Also consider what you realistically have the capacity to build. Think about your available room, time, and budget. A simple window box, a prefabricated structure, or a modest DIY enclosure can be just as rewarding as an elaborate design.

## Start Simple and Grow

You don't need to build your dream catio all at once. Start with a basic, secure structure and add features over time. A catio can be scalable and adaptable to your cat's changing needs—a six-month-old kitten needs different features than a 16-year-old senior cat. You can add more ramps, shelving, or plants as your cat ages.

Bob built a sturdy, wonderful catio for just $125 using basic tools like a hammer, nails, and a glue gun. David created an effective window-box catio that spans windows, giving his cats more sunlight and a view of birds and outdoor activity. Both show that size and complexity don't determine a catio's value—what matters is meeting your cat's and your own needs.

## Aesthetics and Personal Style

Your catio should blend beautifully with your home's existing aesthetic and surroundings, whether you're in nature or a tight-knit community. Diane's catio uses natural wood and nature themes. She even added soft lighting at night to attract insects for her cats to chase, and a hollowed-out log for enrichment. The space includes a midnight curfew when passage in and out is sealed for the night—a feature that works for her cats' routine and her peace of mind.

What makes these spaces special is that they're works in progress. Your catio can evolve as your imagination grows and your cats' needs change.
