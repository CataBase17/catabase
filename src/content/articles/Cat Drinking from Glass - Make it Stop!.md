## Why Your Cat Keeps Dipping Into Your Water Glass

If your cat loves putting his paws and face into your water glass, you're not alone. Many cat owners deal with this frustrating behavior, even when their cats have access to running water fountains or other water sources.

## Understanding Why Cats Do This

Your cat's attraction to your drinking glass likely comes down to a few reasons. First, the glass carries your scent, which makes it appealing to your cat. He may also be drawn to it as a puzzle to solve—something about the challenge of accessing water from a glass appeals to him.

It's worth knowing that a cat's whiskers are equal in length to the width of his body, which helps him navigate tight spaces. Even so, cats will still attempt to drink from glasses despite the potential awkwardness.

Simply telling your cat "no" won't work. You need to redirect his behavior by providing appealing alternatives and making glasses less accessible.

## Solutions That Work

**Create an attractive water station.** Place a decorative water fountain near where you typically sit with your glass—like beside the couch or coffee table. Cats naturally gravitate toward wherever their human family members are, so a fountain in these social spaces will catch his attention and give him an appropriate outlet for his water-drinking urges.

**Offer a shallow bowl alternative.** Set a bowl of water on the table, but don't fill it all the way to the top. This lets your cat dip his head in and drink comfortably without the risk of tipping the bowl over. It satisfies his desire to interact with water while protecting your belongings.

**Keep glasses off the table.** The simplest solution is to not leave glasses sitting out where your cat can access them. This might feel like a compromise, but living with a cat means adjusting your habits.

**Use a coaster.** If you must leave a glass on the table, place a coaster on top of it to make it less attractive and block your cat's access.

## The "No Yes" Approach

Redirect rather than just restrict. When you catch your cat reaching for your glass, say no to that behavior—but immediately offer him a yes in the form of a bowl or fountain nearby. Pair this with the physical barriers like coasters or removing glasses from reach.

Remember, living with a cat is ultimately a life of compromise. By providing attractive water sources in the places where your cat naturally wants to be, you can redirect his behavior and keep your water glass safe.
