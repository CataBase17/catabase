## Communication

Cats have their own language, and showing love to them goes beyond just petting or holding them. In fact, some cats don't enjoy being in your lap or being petted at all. Instead, you can connect with your cat through their eyes and face.

The slow blink, sometimes called the "cat I love you," is a powerful way to communicate affection. Soften your eyes, catch your cat's gaze, and slowly blink. When your cat reciprocates with their own slow blink, they're telling you they trust you—it's their way of saying "I love you" back without words.

You can also greet your cat with a soft clicking sound made with your mouth, similar to how you'd say hello to a friend. Many cats will return this greeting or acknowledge it with a look of recognition.

The "bow" is another subtle gesture you can use. It involves slightly tilting your head or averting your eyes, which creates a sense of equality and mutual respect between you and your cat.

When it comes to physical touch, always ask for permission before petting your cat. Let them make the first move and initiate contact. This respect for their boundaries helps build trust and strengthens your bond.

## Catification

Enriching your cat's environment—a concept called catification—is a meaningful way to show love and care. You can create a home that works for both you and your cat without turning it into a cat house.

Provide vertical spaces where your cat can climb and explore. Cat trees placed near windows are ideal, giving your cat their own elevated perches where they can watch birds and observe the outdoor world. This gives them a sense of ownership over part of their environment.

Make sure your cat has access to multiple comfortable beds and hangout spots throughout your home. These personal spaces are essential for their happiness and sense of security.

Litter boxes deserve special attention too. Instead of hiding them away, place multiple boxes in convenient, accessible locations throughout your home. Easily accessible litter boxes are part of showing your cat that you care about their comfort and well-being. An ideal setup includes having one litter box per cat, plus one extra.

Overall, environmental enrichment means creating a space that stimulates your cat physically and emotionally, meets their needs, and gives them room to express their natural behaviors.

## Playing

Playing with your cat every day is one of the most effective ways to show love and affection. Interactive play allows your cat to express their natural hunting instincts, get exercise, and strengthen the bond between you.

Interactive play—where you hold one end of a toy and your cat pounces on the other end—is particularly powerful. This type of engagement brings out your cat's raw instincts and provides the exercise and mental stimulation they need.

While toys like small furry mice, throw toys, and laser pointers can be fun, they're no substitute for real interactive play with you. True interactive play helps build your cat's confidence and draws out their natural hunting drive, regardless of their age.

Don't assume that older cats won't benefit from play. Cats at any age, whether they're four months or 14 years old, still have the capacity for hunting behavior and can benefit tremendously from daily interactive play sessions.

## Wellness

Showing love to your cat also means prioritizing their overall wellness. One of the most important aspects is nutrition. The quality of food you feed your cat directly affects their happiness and quality of life. Cats thrive on high-quality meat, and feeding them quality food makes a significant difference in their long-term health and happiness.

Regular veterinary check-ups are equally important. Cats are skilled at hiding pain and illness, so yearly exams help uncover hidden health issues early. For example, arthritis affects over 90 percent of senior cats, and your vet can catch this and other age-related conditions before they cause serious problems.

Complementary therapies can also support your cat's wellness. Acupuncture and cold laser therapy are effective options for managing pain and helping older cats stay vital and comfortable in their senior years. Various supplements are also available to support your cat's overall health.

By focusing on nutrition, regular veterinary care, and complementary therapies, you're demonstrating genuine care for your cat and contributing to their happiness and quality of life.

## Friends

Getting another cat is a meaningful way to show love, especially if you have only one cat at home. Most cats don't thrive when they're alone. Having a feline companion helps stave off boredom while you're away and gives your cat someone to interact with and learn from.

Cats are more likely to model behavior from other cats than from humans. When you have multiple cats in your home, they interact with each other, and this social enrichment is beneficial regardless of your current cat's age. A companion can make a significant difference in your cat's overall happiness and well-being.
