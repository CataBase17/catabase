## Toys for Play and Hunting

Play is crucial for your cat's happiness and health, and the right toys bring out their natural hunting instincts while strengthening your bond. Interactive toys like wands with prey attachments let you engage directly with your cat, dragging them along the floor to simulate ground prey or making them fly through the air for aerial hunting. The key is to put these toys away after playtime so your cat feels excited when you bring them out again—similar to the anticipation of opening a can of food. Store them in a cabinet to build that sense of discovery.

Independent toys give your cat something to entertain themselves with throughout the day. These can be batted around, chased, or simply walked away from. Place different independent toys around your house to create a changing landscape that keeps your cat engaged, and having one in your pocket is useful for redirecting your cat away from unwanted behavior. Buy several of these so your cat always has options to explore.

Self-activated toys, whether battery-powered or rechargeable, provide unpredictable movements that keep your cat engaged without your participation. Even just a few minutes of engagement can keep your cat focused and entertained. Once your cat loses interest, pick them up so they don't become background clutter.

Kickers are toys your cat can grab and wrap their paws around to wrestle and release kicking energy. Many are stuffed with catnip, though this isn't necessary for effectiveness. Having multiple kickers around the house gives your cat options throughout their day for solo play and energy release.

## Enrichment Beyond Traditional Toys

Puzzle toys challenge your cat and slow down their eating, since they have to solve a problem to access the food. Many can be adjusted for difficulty level, making them suitable for cats at any skill level. Nose work using snuffle mats lets your cat use their sense of smell to find rewards, much like dogs do—a fun and engaging activity that taps into their natural instincts.

Scratching surfaces are important for your cat's wellbeing and confidence. Provide a variety of surfaces like sisal rope, wood, or carpet to find what your cat prefers. Placing scratching surfaces in areas where your cat can smell and see themselves helps build confidence and encourages regular use. There are many products that bring different facets of exploration, play, and activity into your cat's life every day. This variety helps break routines and promotes creative thinking, allowing your cat to sleep better and feel more fulfilled overall.

## Creating Territory and Resting Spaces

Catification—creating a cat superhighway through your home—lets your cat move from resting place to resting place and claim ownership of spaces by leaving their scent. Understanding your individual cat's preferences is important, as some cats are "bush dwellers" who prefer low, enclosed spaces, while others are "tree dwellers" who love climbing and perching high. Many cats enjoy a mix of both, so the goal is to give your cat options so they can choose what suits them best.

Cat trees and cat condos provide environmental enrichment by allowing your cat to climb, rest, and survey their domain from a position of control. Placing them near a window gives your cat a sense of territory and the ability to observe their surroundings. Window-perched beds combine the features of a cat tree and a bed, providing a vertical resting place where your cat can watch the world outside. Beds, mats, blankets, and cat condos all go beyond basic function—they're spaces where your cat can soak in their scent and feel secure in their environment.

Birdhouses, feeders, and bird baths near windows provide entertainment through bird-watching opportunities, which can be very engaging for your cat. However, balance this passive entertainment with real-life play and interaction with you to keep your cat truly stimulated.

## Herbs and Treats

Not every cat responds to catnip the same way. Some cats may not be affected by it at all, while others might become overly aggressive. If catnip doesn't work for your cat, try alternatives like silver vine, valerian root, or honeysuckle. These herbs are available both in toys and in loose form that you can sprinkle on existing toys. The best approach is to experiment and discover what works best for your individual cat, as trying various options helps you find what brings out the best play behavior in your pet.

Treats are more than just snacks—they're tools for training and deepening your relationship with your cat. Freeze-dried meat treats like 100% turkey breast, chicken, or fish are excellent choices, offering a crunchy texture and simple ingredient lists with nothing artificial. These work wonderfully for clicker training, keeping your cat focused on learning new behaviors, and fit perfectly in puzzle toys for added engagement. Tube treats made by brands like Churu—which are 99% meat in a squeezable tube—let you dispense small amounts to your cat, making them perfect for training and encouraging your cat to follow you. These high-value treats help your cat overcome fears and build confidence while learning new behaviors.

A "jackpot" treat is a special treat that's highly valued by your cat—something they go crazy for. Experimenting with different types and flavors helps you discover what your cat loves most and provides variety that keeps treat time exciting.

## Carriers and Water Fountains

Carriers don't have to be something your cat dreads. Instead of viewing a carrier only as a transport tool for vet visits or car rides, make it a comfortable space where your cat actually wants to go. Some carriers are designed to double as comfortable beds and allow you to zip or remove the top or door for easy conversion between uses. Making your carrier a positive space helps your cat associate it with good things rather than just stressful trips, creating a cozy retreat rather than something your cat fears.

A water fountain provides running water, which many cats find fascinating. It's a great way to encourage hydration while providing entertainment, supporting your cat's natural preferences for moving water.

## Health Supplements

Your cat is an obligate carnivore, meaning they should eat meat and essentially nothing else. Beyond diet itself, certain supplements can boost your cat's health and well-being. Vitamin B12 is particularly valuable, especially in liquid, non-synthetic form. Just a few drops added to your cat's food or water can make a difference. Senior cats benefit from B12 because it can help with cognitive decline and keep their minds sharp. Cats with digestive issues like pancreatitis or irritable bowel disease also benefit from B12 supplementation because it supports gut health. Since gut health is one of the most important factors in your cat's overall wellness, adding B12 may help prevent health problems and reduce vet bills down the road.

## Understanding Your Cat

Beyond physical gifts, understanding your cat's personality and needs is as valuable as any toy or treat—it directly improves your cat's quality of life and the happiness you both share together. Books like "Total Cat Mojo," "Catification," and "Catify to Satisfy" offer comprehensive resources that explore the relationship between you and your cat and provide guidance on strengthening that bond through environmental enrichment and behavioral understanding.
