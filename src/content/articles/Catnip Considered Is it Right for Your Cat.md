## What Is Catnip?

Catnip is an herb in the mint family, but not all cats respond to it equally. About 30% of cats don't react to catnip at all due to genetic predisposition. The ingredient responsible for a cat's response is nepetalactone, an oil in the plant that triggers a reaction in genetically sensitive cats.

Male cats are more likely to respond to catnip because the smell of nepetalactone mimics that of female urine. However, every cat's individual reaction varies widely. Some cats become happy and playful, while others may turn aggressive or bullying. Some become more fearful. It's important to observe how your own cat responds.

The potency of catnip differs depending on where it comes from. Catnip grown in a home garden tends to be milder than store-bought catnip, which becomes more potent when crushed or ground since this releases more of the active compound.

The effects of catnip typically last 10 to 15 minutes, and after exposure, it takes a couple of hours before your cat becomes sensitive to it again. Your cat cannot overdose on catnip. To keep the experience exciting for your cat, use catnip as an occasional treat or special event rather than a regular part of their routine.

## Age and Other Factors

Young kittens don't typically respond to catnip—most cats don't start reacting until around 6 months old. Elderly cats are also less likely to have a strong reaction.

Interestingly, big cats like tigers, lions, and bobcats also respond to catnip, though only a percentage of them are affected, just like domestic cats.

## Alternatives to Catnip

If your cat doesn't respond to catnip, several alternatives exist. Silver Vine is increasingly available and works on about 80% of cats, compared to the 68% that respond to catnip. Even better, about 75% of cats that don't respond to catnip will respond to Silver Vine.

Valerian root is another option, though it has a strong, unpleasant smell to humans. Many cats love it, though the exact percentage that responds is unclear. Tartarian honeysuckle is also popular with cats.

Rotating between different stimulants—catnip, Silver Vine, Valerian root, and Tartarian honeysuckle—keeps your cat engaged and provides variety. However, be cautious when introducing new plants or oils to your cat, as some can be toxic. Do thorough research before trying anything new.
