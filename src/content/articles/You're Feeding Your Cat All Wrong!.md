## Why Scheduled Meals Are Better Than Free Feeding

Your cat's natural eating pattern involves small meals spread throughout the day—ideally every five to six hours. Free feeding, which makes food available around the clock, works against this instinct and can lead to physical and behavioral problems.

When you switch to scheduled mealtimes, you gain a major advantage: you can anticipate when your cat will need to use the litter box. This makes it much easier to identify and address litter box issues, since you'll have a clearer picture of your cat's bathroom habits. Free feeding obscures these patterns and can make behavioral problems harder to diagnose.

## Using Food as Motivation

Unlike dogs, cats aren't naturally motivated to please their owners. This means you need to find other ways to motivate them—and food is one of the most effective tools you have. When you move away from free feeding and onto a meal schedule, you create opportunities to use food as a reward. This gives you a practical way to work with your cat's natural instincts rather than against them.

## The Benefits of Mealtime Feeding

Switching from free feeding to scheduled meals can help you address various behavioral and health issues. By controlling when your cat eats, you gain more insight into their needs and create a framework for positive change. Getting your cat off the free feeding habit and onto a regular mealtime schedule is one of the most straightforward ways to improve both their health and behavior.
