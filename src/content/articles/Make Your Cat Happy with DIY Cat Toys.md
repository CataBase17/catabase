## How to Make a Kicker

A kicker is one of the easiest DIY toys you can make for your cat, and it requires just a few materials: a sock, scissors, catnip, polyfill, and brown paper.

Start by stuffing the sock with layers of polyfill and crumpled brown paper. The paper adds texture and makes a satisfying sound when your cat plays with it. As you build up the layers, sprinkle catnip throughout—think of it like making a seven-layer dip, alternating between filling and catnip. Continue until the sock is full, leaving about 3 to 4 inches empty at the top.

Once stuffed, cut straight down about 3 inches from the top of the filling. Then cut fringe all the way around the sock, making strips about 3/4 of an inch wide. To seal it, grab two opposite fringe strips and tie them in a knot, pulling tight. Tie a double knot on top, then repeat with two other opposite strips.

The result is a toy with a fun fringe end that your cat will love to kick and carry around. You can make kickers in different sizes—use a longer sock for a bigger toy or just the foot of the sock for a smaller one. It's a great way to repurpose old socks.

## How to Make a Wand Toy

A wand toy is interactive and engaging, and you'll need a 12-inch dowel (1/4-inch wide), fleece fabric, ribbon about an inch wide, scissors, a ruler, a lighter, and a hot glue gun with glue sticks.

Start by finding the direction of the fleece that stretches less—this will be the direction you cut. Cut the fleece into half-inch wide strips, but not all the way across. Leave about 1 inch at the top (roughly the width of your ribbon). This creates a hoola skirt-like effect. A cutting mat with a grid helps ensure your cuts are straight and even.

Once the fleece is prepared, attach the dowel to the top of the strips using hot glue. Use just a small dot of glue on the edge of the top part—being sparing with glue prevents it from squishing out and potentially causing burns.

Next, cut a piece of ribbon 1 inch wide and 4 inches long. Seal the edges with a lighter by running the flame quickly along each edge to prevent fraying. Attach this ribbon to the base of the wand using hot glue, which adds both a decorative touch and extra support. Start with a little glue on one end of the ribbon and let it dry before wrapping it around the base. The finished wand should have the ribbon covering the bottom of the fleece, and it's ready for interactive play.

## How to Make a Puzzle Toy

Puzzle toys provide wonderful mental and physical stimulation for your cat. For this project, gather a shoe box, empty paper tubes (toilet paper or paper towel tubes), scissors, and a hot glue gun.

Arrange the paper tubes inside the shoe box. If they don't fit together perfectly, use glue to hold them in place. You can use bigger paper towel tubes and cut them to different heights, creating variable difficulty levels. The shoe box lid can also be positioned to add more height variety. If there's extra space between tubes, the glue gun will secure them so they don't shift around when your cat plays.

The real magic of puzzle toys happens when you fill the tubes with treats. Your cat has to figure out how to extract them, which challenges both mind and body. You can use store-bought treats or homemade options like freeze-dried meat treats. The difficulty level can be adjusted based on your cat's age, dexterity, and size, so you can customize the toy to match your individual cat's needs and preferences.

Puzzle toys are highly effective enrichment tools, and the simple fact that they're made from recyclable materials makes them budget-friendly to refresh and change out regularly.
