## What is Catification?

Catification is the art of designing your home to work for both you and your cats. It's about creating an enriched space where neither you nor your cat has to sacrifice comfort—instead, you build an environment where everyone thrives together. The term works as a noun and a verb: your home is "catified," and the act of improving it for your feline companion is "catifying."

The key to successful catification is understanding two things: what cats need in general, and who your specific cat is as an individual. Every cat has their own personality and preferences, so the approach that works for one cat might not work for another. What matters most is that catification feels sustainable for you. If you resent giving up too much room for litter boxes or cat furniture, you're less likely to stick with it. The solution is integrating these items creatively into your rooms so they feel like part of your home's design rather than an eyesore. A litter box tucked into a corner with thoughtful styling or a scratcher that complements your décor feels very different from a cat box sitting in the middle of your living room.

## Building Your Cat's Confidence

Your cat develops confidence and a sense of ownership through what's called "cat mojo"—their feeling of security and territorial control. In a home with multiple people, pets, or even just one owner, your cat might not feel like they truly own anything unless you give them space to claim. The good news is that building mojo is straightforward once you understand how your cat sees the world.

Cats naturally seek out the vertical world. By owning the heights in your home, your cat claims territory that others are unlikely to challenge. This mirrors how their wild ancestors established confidence by marking and controlling their territory. However, not all cats are equally comfortable at height. Some cats are "bush dwellers" who feel confident on the floor, while others are "tree dwellers" who need to be up high. Still others are most comfortable on a couch or other elevated furniture. The key is observing your cat to discover where they transform into their best self. You might notice your cat looks small and scared on the floor but becomes bold and comfortable when elevated. Once you identify your cat's confident where, you can build their environment around it.

This means creating "cat superhighways"—pathways at their preferred height that let them move around the room without touching the floor. You can incorporate different types of furniture, cozy spots where they can cocoon and feel safe, and areas that offer a gentle challenge, all arranged at their confident level. Simple ideas work wonderfully. A cardboard box becomes a cozy bed. A shelf attached to the side of your couch creates a cat superhighway where your cat can jump from place to place. These small touches give your cat places to rest, perch, and feel secure without requiring an elaborate or expensive setup.

## Scent Soakers and Territorial Markers

Your cat builds mojo through scent association. Items like beds, cat trees, blankets, and scratching posts act as "scent soakers," allowing your cat to leave their scent and feel ownership. Cats define their sense of belonging by soaking their scent into objects throughout your home, much like you use pictures and personal items to mark your space and feel ownership. When your cat lies on or rubs against these items, they're marking territory and creating a sense of home.

Place multiple blankets, beds, and other soft objects around your house at your cat's preferred height where they can leave their scent. Every bed, tree, and scratcher you add creates a stronger sense of belonging for your cat. Litter boxes are the most important scent and visual markers for your cat, as they leave both scent and visual marks through their claws. Having enough boxes placed strategically throughout your home is crucial. Don't hide litter boxes in unlikely places like behind the washing machine in the garage. Instead, place them in socially significant areas of your home. Proper placement prevents many behavioral problems, including cats peeing outside the box, fighting with other cats, and general anxiety. When litter boxes are well-placed, your cat feels confident marking territory and feels secure in their space.

## Window Entertainment and Passive Enrichment

Cats spend significant time looking out windows, and you can enhance this natural behavior by creating what amounts to cat TV. Place a bird feeder outside the window to create an entertaining experience, or if that's not feasible, consider an aquarium with artificial fish and other sea creatures. This lets your cat engage their hunting instincts without actually hunting.

You can also place cat trees or window sill beds in your windows so your cat has a comfortable spot to watch the outside world while enjoying their favorite perch height. Try to create a cat-friendly feature at every window in your house. This passive engagement keeps your cat calm and content while you're away, so they're relaxed rather than an uncontained ball of energy when you return.

## Outdoor Enrichment Options

If you have the space, creating a catio—an enclosed outdoor space—is a wonderful addition, especially for households with multiple cats. A catio can be as simple as enclosing a back patio or using outdoor playpen kits. A catio gives your cats access to fresh air, sunlight, and the chance to hunt bugs, all while keeping them safe from the outside world and from harming wildlife. Even small outdoor spaces can be utilized effectively to provide this enrichment.

## Starting Your Catification Journey

The most important thing to remember is that catification doesn't need to be fancy or expensive. What matters is that it works for both you and your cat. Some people create elaborate cat highways and structures, while others keep things simple—both approaches work equally well.

Don't wait for the perfect plan or the budget to do everything at once. Begin with whatever you have right now. Every small addition—a bed, a scratcher, a new perch—creates mojo for your cat and shows them you're building a space that belongs to them. Big plans sometimes never happen, but small, immediate changes do. Your cat will appreciate a modest catified space today far more than they'll appreciate elaborate plans that keep getting postponed. Start wherever you are, with whatever resources you have, and build from there.
