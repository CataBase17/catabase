## Foods That Are Toxic to Cats

Your cat's digestive system evolved specifically to process prey—meat, bones, muscle, and fat. As an obligate carnivore, your cat's biology is finely tuned for this narrow diet, and their digestive system, down to their blood cells, is fragile when exposed to foods outside this range. This is why certain foods are absolute dangers.

**Alcohol** is completely off-limits. Treat access to alcohol the same way you'd protect a child—keep it locked away where your cat cannot reach it.

**Caffeine** found in coffee, tea, and energy drinks is toxic to cats and can harm their sensitive digestive system. Keep all caffeinated beverages out of reach.

**Chocolate** is toxic for two reasons: it's a stimulant, and it often contains xylitol, an artificial sweetener that's harmful to cats. Be aware that xylitol appears in unexpected places, including some chewing gums.

**Onions and garlic** are dangerous because they disrupt the delicate balance of your cat's kidneys, liver, and blood cells. Keep all allium vegetables away from your cat.

## Foods That Aren't Suitable for Cats

Most **baked goods**, including bread, contain baking soda, baking powder, and yeast. These ingredients can expand in your cat's digestive system and affect blood circulation.

**Dairy products** like milk, cheese, and cream should be avoided. Cats are lactose intolerant and cannot digest these foods—only their mother's milk or specialized milk replacers are appropriate for kittens.

**Grapes and raisins** are toxic to dogs and should be kept away from cats as a precaution, given their sensitive digestive systems.

**Nutmeg** is toxic to cats, and excessive salt or other spices should be avoided as well. Your cat's system simply isn't designed to tolerate these seasonings.

## Foods to Limit or Approach with Caution

**Avocado** flesh isn't toxic in small amounts, but excessive consumption can become toxic. The bark and skin of avocados are outright toxic and should never be given to your cat.

**Fat** requires careful attention. While cats are obligate carnivores and do need fat, excessive amounts—like large quantities of fatty beef—can cause pancreatitis and should be avoided.

**Tuna** contains mercury, which is present in nearly all farm-raised tuna in the US food supply. Occasional tuna treats are acceptable, but tuna shouldn't become a regular part of your cat's diet.

**Raw fish** can be problematic due to mercury content and thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency issues. Like tuna, it should only be offered occasionally and in moderation.

## What You Should Know

There are over 450 types of plants alone that can be harmful to cats. Because your cat is an obligate carnivore, their body can only process what they evolved to eat—and their tolerance for anything else is extremely limited. Some foods like avocado or fat aren't toxic in small doses, but many others can cause serious harm. Understanding the difference is essential to keeping your cat safe.
