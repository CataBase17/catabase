## Understanding Cat Nutrition

Cats are obligate carnivores, which means their bodies are designed to eat meat. This is a fundamental fact that shapes everything you should know about feeding your cat well. Cats have no nutritional requirement for carbohydrates—their systems evolved to thrive on a raw meat diet. When you feed your cat foods that don't match this evolutionary need, you're setting them up for potential health problems.

## What a Raw Meat Diet Does for Your Cat

When cats switch to a raw meat diet, the changes are noticeable. You'll likely see increased energy levels, a softer and silkier coat, and a significant reduction in fecal odor. These improvements happen even in young, healthy cats, which shows that a raw diet benefits all cats, not just those with existing health issues.

Many cat owners have reported that their veterinarians are impressed by their cats' health after observing the results of a raw meat diet. Eighteen-year-old cats on this diet often appear unusually healthy for their age. When owners explain they're feeding raw meat, some vets begin to reconsider their previous assumptions about feline nutrition.

## The Problem with Dry Food

Dry food is problematic for cats because it's filled with carbohydrates—even grain-free varieties. Manufacturers of grain-free dry food simply substitute grains with other carbohydrates like vegetables, which cats don't need and which don't provide the moisture content your cat's body requires.

Since cats evolved to eat raw meat with high moisture content, dry food can contribute to dehydration and related health issues. Many cats may seem fine on dry food for years, but underlying problems like kidney disease or obesity can develop silently. While some cats do live into their 20s on dry food, this doesn't mean the diet is healthy for them—it just means those particular cats happened to survive it. You can't predict which cats will develop problems or when, making it a risk that isn't worth taking.

## Building a Proper Raw Diet

A raw meat diet isn't just plain meat. Cats need meat, bone, and organ to get complete nutrition. Plain muscle meat alone would be deficient. If you're interested in feeding raw, you can follow a recipe to ensure your cat gets all the nutrients they need.

If you're not ready to switch to raw food, at minimum consider feeding your cat a grain-free wet diet rather than dry food. This gives your cat the moisture and meat-based nutrition they need more than kibble provides.
