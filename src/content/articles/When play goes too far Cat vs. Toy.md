## Playing Safely With a Cat Who Swallows Toys

If your cat has a tendency to try swallowing toys during playtime, you're dealing with a cat who has strong natural hunting instincts. The good news is that you can satisfy these instincts while keeping him safe—it just takes some planning and awareness.

## Understanding Two Types of Toys

There are two main categories of cat toys to know about. Interactive toys are ones where you hold one end and your cat holds the other, so you're playing together. Remote toys are ones your cat plays with on his own. The critical rule for both types: never leave either one lying around for your cat to find when you're not supervising. Put them away immediately after playtime ends.

## The Right Way to Play With Interactive Toys

When you're playing with an interactive toy, watch your cat carefully. Cats naturally want to grab their "prey" and hold it, but they'll eventually release their grip when they check to see if what they've caught is dead. That moment is your opportunity. As soon as you feel him loosen his hold, pull the toy away and keep the game going. This prevents him from getting a chance to swallow it.

## Complete the Hunting Cycle

Your cat's natural instinct follows a sequence: hunt, catch, kill, eat. To truly satisfy this drive and prevent him from trying to swallow toys, have a meal ready for him right after playtime. This completes the cycle and gives him the reward he's naturally seeking. A well-fed, satisfied cat is much less likely to be driven to swallow toys.

## Plan Your Play Sessions

For safe, successful playtime, you need a clear plan. Know when you're starting play and when you'll stop. Keep both interactive and remote toys stored away when you're not actively using them. Stay in control throughout the session so you can manage the toy and prevent swallowing. With this structure in place, you can give your cat the hunting satisfaction he needs while keeping him safe and healthy.
