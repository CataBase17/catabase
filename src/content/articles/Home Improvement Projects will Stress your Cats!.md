## Preparing Your Cats for Construction and Major Disruptions

If your home is about to undergo construction or major renovations, your cats will need extra support to stay calm and safe during the chaos. Here's how to help them cope with the noise, unfamiliar people, and disrupted routines.

## Create a Base Camp

The most important step is to establish a dedicated safe space—a "base camp"—where your cats can retreat during construction. This should be one quiet room, away from the main work area if possible. Stock it with everything your cats need: food bowls, water bowls, litter boxes, cat trees, and cat beds.

The key to making this space comforting is filling it with familiar scents. Include blankets, bedding, and cat furniture that smell like you and your cats. These scent-soaked items help your cats maintain their sense of territory and identity, which is especially important during stressful times. In a multi-cat household, the shared group scent becomes even more valuable—construction workers bringing unfamiliar smells into your home can compromise this sense of security, so the base camp becomes a refuge where your cats' familiar scent world is preserved.

During the day while construction is happening, keep your cats in the base camp. This might feel like a lockdown, but it's actually protecting them from stress and potential dangers like open doors or unfamiliar people. Your cats can find comfort in each other's company and in the familiar surroundings. You can visit them during the day to maintain a sense of normalcy and reassurance. When the workers leave for the day, open up the base camp and let your cats explore the rest of the house again.

## For Outdoor Cats During Construction

If your kittens or cats go outside, construction creates a real safety risk. The noise and commotion can frighten them, and frightened cats sometimes don't return home. During active construction, it's safest to keep outdoor cats indoors. This temporary change protects them from the stress and the risk of getting lost or injured.

## After Construction Ends

Once the construction is complete, the base camp helps your cats rebuild their confidence. They can gradually reacquaint themselves with the rest of the house at their own pace, replacing any negative associations with the chaos they experienced with positive ones as they reclaim their territory room by room.
