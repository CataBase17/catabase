## Why Your Kitten Plays with Litter

If your kitten is using the litter box properly but then going back to play with the litter afterward, you're witnessing completely normal kitten behavior. At around five months old, kittens are in the height of their playful phase and view essentially everything—including litter—as a toy. Your kitten isn't doing anything wrong; he's just exploring his world the way kittens do.

When your kitten pulls out little clumps of litter to bat around and play with, he's mesmerized by the texture and movement. This behavior isn't something you need to worry about. As your kitten matures, he'll naturally outgrow this fascination with litter, just as he would lose interest in any other toy.

## Redirecting Your Kitten's Energy

The key to minimizing the mess is to offer activities that are even more fun than playing with litter. Since everything is a discovery for kittens at this stage, you have plenty of options to capture his attention.

Tunnels and boxes work wonderfully. A cat tunnel or even a cardboard delivery box can become endlessly entertaining if you put toys inside. Kittens love pulling things out of other things, so watch as your kitten chases and bats toys around inside the tunnel or box, then retrieves them. This mimics the same pulling and extracting action that makes the litter so appealing, but directs it toward something cleaner.

Food puzzle toys are excellent for kittens of any age. Fill them with treats or kibble and let your kitten figure out how to extract the food. This provides both mental stimulation and entertainment. Blankets with hidden toys tucked inside can also encourage exploration and play.

The experience becomes even more rewarding when you're involved. Your kitten picks up on your tone and excitement, and interactive play strengthens your bond. By trying different toys and activities, you'll discover what your kitten enjoys most and can tailor playtime accordingly.

The goal isn't just to stop the litter play—it's to give your kitten better alternatives while building a strong connection with him. Once you find the right activities, the litter box will become boring in comparison.
